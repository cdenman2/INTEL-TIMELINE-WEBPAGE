1968

Intel Founded

Intel was founded by Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore, who worked together at Fairchild Semiconductors based in San Jose, California, with the determination to make memory chips smaller, dependable, and high performance.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel produced and Introduced the first commercial microprocessor which was the start of innovation for the modern world. It was a 4 bit processor and the microprocessor was discontinued in 1982.

1978

Intel 8086

Intel introduces the Intel 8086 Microprocessor which started the X86 innovation into personal computers with a 16 bit processor. It also should state that it was discontinued in 1998.

1985

32-bit Processor (386)

Intel introduces the 32 bit processor with 275K transistors which was incorporated into many Personal Computers. It should also state that it was discontinued in 2007.

2006

Core 2 Duo

Intel introduces the Intel Core 2 Duo Microprocessor which was a 64 bit that increased the power consumption and boosted performance which was a major asset in computing on Personal Computers and Laptops. It should also state that it was discontinued in 2008.

2020

2020 Product Advances

Intel introduces a few products including the Intel Atom PS900, the Intel 3rd Gen Xeon Scalable, and the 10nm-based 11th Gen Intel Core. Intel Atom PS900 Microprocessor that was primarily built for low power consumption and embedding file applications on netbooks. The Intel 3rd Gen Xeon Scalable Microprocessor could achieve 80 cores and was built for high workloads and performance with power efficiency. The Intel 11th Gen Intel Core was built for high resolution with graphics. All three processors are still being used to this day and not discontinued. Another fact is that Intel was at the frontlines of AI and 5g Production.
