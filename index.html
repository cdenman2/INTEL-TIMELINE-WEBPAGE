Timeline

1968
Intel Founded
Intel was founded by Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore, who worked together at Fairchild Semiconductors based in San Jose, California, with the determination to make memory chips smaller, dependable, and high performance.
1971
First Microprocessor
Model: Intel 4004
Intel produced and introduced the first commercial microprocessor, the Intel 4004. It was a 4-bit processor that helped spark modern computing innovation. The Intel 4004 was discontinued in 1982.
1978
Intel 8086
Model: Intel 8086
Intel introduces the Intel 8086 microprocessor which started the x86 innovation into personal computers with a 16-bit processor. It was discontinued in 1998.
1985
32-bit Processor (386)
Model: Intel 80386
Intel introduces the 32-bit processor, the Intel 80386, containing 275K transistors and incorporated into many personal computers. It was discontinued in 2007.
2006
Intel Core 2 Duo
Model: Intel Core 2 Duo
Intel introduces the Intel Core 2 Duo microprocessor, a 64-bit CPU that improved performance and efficiency for personal computers and laptops. It was discontinued in 2008.
2020
2020 Product Advances
Status: Not Discontinued
Intel introduces products including the Intel Atom PS900, the 3rd Gen Xeon Scalable, and the 10nm-based 11th Gen Intel Core. These processors focus on low power consumption, high workloads, and high-resolution graphics. Intel was also at the frontlines of AI and 5G production.
