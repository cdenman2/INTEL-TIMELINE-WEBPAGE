Timeline

1968
Intel Founded
Intel was founded by Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore, who worked together at Fairchild Semiconductors based in San Jose, California, with the determination to make memory chips smaller, dependable, and high performance.
1971
First Microprocessor
Model: Intel 4004
Intel introduces the Intel 4004 Microprocessor, the first commercial microprocessor. It was a 4-bit processor and the microprocessor was discontinued in 1982.
1978
Intel 8086
Model: Intel 8086
Intel introduces the Intel 8086 Microprocessor which started the X86 innovation into personal computers with a 16-bit processor. It was discontinued in 1998.
1985
Intel 80386
Intel introduces the 32-bit processor with 275K transistors which was incorporated into many Personal Computers. It was discontinued in 2007.
2006
Intel Core 2 Duo
Intel introduces the Intel Core 2 Duo Microprocessor which was a 64-bit that increased the power consumption and boosted performance which was a major asset in computing on Personal Computers and Laptops. It was discontinued in 2008.
2020
2020 Product Advances
Intel introduces a few products including the Intel Atom PS900, the Intel 3rd Gen Xeon Scalable, and the 10nm-based 11th Gen Intel Core.

Intel Atom PS900: primarily built for low power consumption and embedding file applications on netbooks.
Intel 3rd Gen Xeon Scalable: could achieve 80 cores and was built for high workloads and performance with power efficiency.
Intel 11th Gen Intel Core: built for high resolution with graphics.

All three processors are still being used to this day and not discontinued. Another fact is that Intel was at the frontlines of AI and 5G production.
