Timeline

1968

Intel Founded

Intel was founded by Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore, who worked together at Fairchild Semiconductors based in San Jose, California, with the determination to make memory chips smaller, dependable and high performance.

1971

First Microprocessor

Model: Intel 4004

Intel produced and introduced the first commercial microprocessor (Intel 4004), a 4-bit processor that helped spark modern computing innovation. The Intel 4004 was discontinued in 1982.

1978

Intel 8086

Model: Intel 8086

Intel introduces the Intel 8086 Microprocessor which started the x86 innovation into personal computers with a 16-bit processor. It was discontinued in 1998.

1985

Intel 80386

Model: Intel 80386

Intel introduces the 32-bit processor (Intel 80386) with 275K transistors, incorporated into many personal computers. It was discontinued in 2007.

2006

Intel Core 2 Duo

Model: Intel Core 2 Duo

Intel introduces the Intel Core 2 Duo Microprocessor, a 64-bit CPU that improved performance and efficiency for personal computers and laptops. It was discontinued in 2008.

2020

2020 Product Advances

Intel introduces products including the Intel Atom, 3rd Gen Xeon Scalable, and the 10nm-based 11th Gen Intel Core. Intel continues advancing CPUs for laptops, servers, and graphics.
