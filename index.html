INTEL'S PRODUCTION AND INNOVATION THROUGH THE YEARS.

With modern technology on the rise, Intel is staying a step ahead to ensure we are in sync with the future.

Timeline

1968

Intel Founded

Intel was founded by Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore, who worked together at Fairchild Semiconductors in San Jose, California, with the determination to make memory chips smaller, dependable, and high performance.

1971

First Microprocessor

Model: Intel 4004

Intel introduced the Intel 4004, the first commercial microprocessor. It was a 4-bit processor that helped spark modern computing innovation. The Intel 4004 was discontinued in 1982.

1978

Intel 8086

Model: Intel 8086

Intel introduced the Intel 8086 microprocessor, starting the x86 innovation into personal computers with a 16-bit processor. It was discontinued in 1998.

1985

Intel 80386

Model: Intel 80386

Intel introduced the Intel 80386 (386), a major 32-bit processor generation that powered many personal computers. It was discontinued in 2007.

2006

Intel Core 2 Duo

Model: Intel Core 2 Duo

Intel introduced the Intel Core 2 Duo CPU, improving performance and efficiency for desktops and laptops. It was discontinued in 2008.

2020

2020 Product Advances

Still Active: Not discontinued

Intel introduced products including the Intel Atom P5900, 3rd Gen Xeon Scalable, and 10nm-based 11th Gen Intel Core. These advances improved low-power computing, server performance, and integrated graphics.
