Timeline

1968

Intel Founded

Intel was founded by Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore, who worked together at Fairchild Semiconductors in San Jose, California, with the determination to make memory chips smaller, dependable, and high performance.

1971

First Microprocessor

Model: Intel 4004

Intel produced and introduced the first commercial microprocessor (Intel 4004), a 4-bit processor that helped spark modern computing innovation. The Intel 4004 was discontinued in 1982.

1978

Intel 8086

Model: Intel 8086

Intel introduces the Intel 8086 microprocessor, starting the x86 innovation into personal computers with a 16-bit processor. It was discontinued in 1998.

1985

Intel 80386

Model: Intel 80386

Intel introduces the Intel 80386 processor with 275K transistors, incorporated into many personal computers. It was discontinued in 2007.

2006

Intel Core 2 Duo

Model: Intel Core 2 Duo

Intel introduces the Intel Core 2 Duo microprocessor, a 64-bit CPU that improved performance and efficiency for personal computers and laptops. It was discontinued in 2008.

2020

2020 Product Advances

Still Active: Not discontinued

Intel introduces products including the Intel Atom P5900, 3rd Gen Xeon Scalable, and 10nm-based 11th Gen Intel Core. All three are still being used today and are not discontinued.
